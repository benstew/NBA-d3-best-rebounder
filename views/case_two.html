Second Action Point: Following His Shot

"fol-oh-ing hiz SHot": The ability of a player to follow their missed field goal attempt. This will be measured by the player's ability to head towards the basket immediately after shooting. They will be expected to run towards the basket until they meet resistence. If this is the case, they will achieve a full score.

Analysis

Andre Drummond is a very talented, young basketball player. I had the privilige of playing with him in college and it is great to see the transformation that he has made. He is an ideal big man that will bring any team rebounds, blocks, scoring in the paint. Due to Andre's dominance on the inside, he gathers most of his points close to the basket. 94.2%* of his shots are within 10 feet of the basket. This shows that he relies on close range points to fill up his scoring quotas. He is shooting 53.5% from the field which is pretty good but it is clear to see where he is earning his paycheck.

Even though Andre Drummond is leading the league in rebounding, he can increase his statistics if he begins following his own jump shot more regularly. 87.4%* of his rebounds come within six feet of the basket and since he is shooting about 1 for 2 from close to the same distance, he can definitely add to his numbers. He's averaging around 18.2 points per game so if we ignore free throws, it is safe to assume, that he is giving away 3-6 offensive rebounds a game to someone else. That is incredible. Simply by following his shot, he could easily increase his rebounds per game by 1-2.

The above chart shows the shots he missed during the December 14th game against the Los Angeles Clippers. I have adopted the use of a z-scale to determine efficiency. The scale is simply based on the players ability to follow their jumpshot until they hit any resistence. If the actively pursuit thier shot, then they will be awared a +1 and if they don't, then they will lose out on this point with a score of -1. Missed alley oops, dunks, and layups will be excluded from the chart because, those are more of a second jump effort as opposed to activey following the shot.
